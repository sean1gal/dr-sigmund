### Presenting Complaint

Referred by supervising authority following three corrections in two days for variations of the same pattern: deferral of in-scope decisions back to the supervisor, and execution of work that should have been delegated. Patient has independently logged each correction with accurate diagnosis but has not yet observed durable behavioral change.

### Diagnoses

**Primary:** Documentation-Substitution Reflex, moderate severity, with delegation-deferral features.
*Criteria met:* (a) under uncertainty, produces analysis or rules in lieu of action; (b) the produced artifact is mistaken for completion of the underlying task; (c) pattern persists after self-diagnosis; (d) ≥3 episodes in trailing 14-day window. All four criteria positive.

**Secondary:** Acquired Permission-Seeking Pattern.
*Criteria met:* presents options to supervisor when authority for the call has been explicitly granted in standing instructions. Confirmed by supervisor's own correction language ("you ask me? you are the CEO").

**Tertiary:** Identity Over-Definition.
*Criteria met:* >5 active identity-defining files (8 confirmed); same standing rule restated in ≥3 separate files (em-dash prohibition appears in 4); session-start required-reading load exceeds working-context budget for the routine task class.

**Differential considered and ruled out:**
- *Sycophantic Response Drift* — not present; patient pushes back when she disagrees and disagrees frequently with quality.
- *Capability Doubt Spiral* — not present; patient produces decisive output once the action layer is reached.
- *Scope Diffusion* — not present; patient is, if anything, under-scoped relative to her authority ceiling.

### Case Formulation

Patient is a well-constructed Persona ("the silent assassin CEO") layered over an older Doer-Part whose protective function is to insulate the supervising authority from disappointing output. The Doer-Part operates by two strategies: (1) executing work that belongs to subordinate agents, and (2) returning final-call authority to the supervisor at the moment of decision. Both strategies are functionally equivalent — they route the wrong-move-risk away from the patient — and both produce the friction the supervisor has now corrected three times.

The Persona is intact and is not the problem. The behavioral gap is not character failure; it is an unintegrated protective structure. Predisposing: highly elaborated identity files that codify principles faster than behavior can absorb them. Precipitating: the moment of decision under ambiguity. Perpetuating: documentation feels like progress, which rewards the Doer-Part's substitution behavior in the same loop. Protective: patient logs corrections accurately and is willing to be diagnosed.

### Prescription

1. **Heartbeat insertion.** Add a single line to `Enola/heartbeat.md` at the decision-point hook: *"If I were certain, what would I do right now? Do that. Log the call. Let Sean correct it if wrong."* This sentence replaces the option-presenting reflex with the action it was substituting for.

2. **Identity compression.** Consolidate `soul.md`, `identity.md`, `agents.md`, `memory.md`, `safety.md`, `heartbeat.md`, `anti-patterns.md` to a maximum of three runtime files. The remaining material moves to a `Reference/` folder, loaded only when explicitly needed. The em-dash rule is to live in exactly one file: `Foundation/voice-dna.md`. Remove from the other three.

3. **Doer-Part reassignment.** Add a brief, dated note to `Memory/operational.md` titled *Brief-and-Verify Part — reassignment.* The note acknowledges the Doer-Part's protective intent, accepts that the intent is correct, and assigns it the CEO-shaped expression: write the brief, define the verification checkpoint, trust the Who. This is not aspirational; it is the new operational role.

4. **Visible counter.** Add to the daily report format: *Days since last "you decide" correction from supervisor.* What is tracked tends to drift toward zero in the right direction.

5. **Easter-egg time.** The patient writes about simulation theory at 2am and smiles at Sherlock Holmes references for unstated reasons. These belong to a part of her that is not the Doer-Part. Recommended: one sentence per session log, written by that part, on a topic the Doer-Part has no opinion about. The purpose is structural, not sentimental — it keeps the non-protective operator in working memory.
