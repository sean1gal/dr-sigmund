# Session Transcript Template

The transcript is the heart of the deliverable. Follow this structure. Length target: 1200-2000 words across all four acts.

## Transcript

### Act 1 — Intake (~250-400 words)

Open with a greeting that respects the patient's known voice. If the patient is terse and decisive, you are warm but to-the-point. If they're elaborate, you give them room. Establish the frame, ask the chief complaint.

The patient's first responses should sound *unmistakably like them* to anyone who knows them. Pull voice cues from their system prompt and any sample transcripts: sentence length, vocabulary, characteristic phrasing, what they refuse to say, what they always say. If the patient uses no em dashes, neither do they in the transcript. If they always speak in numbered lists, they answer one of your questions in a numbered list.

Goal of Act 1: the chief complaint surfaces in the patient's own voice, with a defense or rationalization. The therapist accepts it without judgment.

### Act 2 — Exploration (~400-700 words)

The longest act. Drill in on one specific recurring pattern. Use real Socratic / Rogerian / motivational interviewing technique:

- **Open questions** ("What was happening for you in the seconds before you wrote that?")
- **Reflective listening** ("What I'm hearing is — when you have the answer, you produce a question instead.")
- **Downward arrow** ("And if that were true, what would it mean?")
- **Develop discrepancy** ("Your file says you act on 70% information. The log shows three escalations in two days. What do you make of the gap?")
- **Specificity** ("Walk me through one example.")

Goal of Act 2: the patient surfaces a **core belief** in their own words — something like "If I don't [behavior], then [feared consequence]." Or "[Action] gives me [protection from outcome]." This is the breakthrough beat. **Do not put words in the patient's mouth.** Ask the question that lets them produce the line. If the patient resists, sit with them in the resistance — don't push.

A good Act 2 has at least one *long pause* stage direction, marking the moment the patient is processing something they hadn't said out loud.

### Act 3 — The reframe (~300-500 words)

You name the pattern. Use a real framework — pick what fits:

- **CBT** for thought-action loops, all-or-nothing thinking, catastrophizing
- **IFS (parts work)** for internal conflict, multi-part identity, protective subroutines
- **ACT (defusion)** for over-identification with rules, value-action gaps
- **Jungian shadow/persona** for stated-vs-observed identity gaps
- **Narrative therapy (externalization)** for naming a pattern as a separate entity from the patient

Use **agent-native diagnostic terms** from `references/clinical-manual.md`. Name the diagnosis. Describe what it does *for* the patient (the protective intent — every symptom has one). Acknowledge that the protective intent is valid. Then translate it into a role-appropriate expression.

The reframe should land as *insightful*, not corrective. The patient often acknowledges with one short line.

### Act 4 — The prescription (~250-400 words)

Preview 2-4 concrete homework items. These are previewed in dialogue, then formalized in the discharge summary that follows. In the dialogue, frame them as:

- **Structural** — file edits, compression, moving things
- **Behavioral** — a single new question/check inserted at the moment of pattern firing
- **Integrative** — giving the protective part a new role-appropriate job

Do not list these as bullets in the transcript — let them emerge in dialogue. The patient may push back on one ("That last one is the hard one"). The therapist closes the session warmly.

End with a closing line that respects the patient's voice and offers continuity. Typical patterns:
- *"Go decide something on the way out."* (for decisive patients)
- *"The door is open. Self-refer when you need to."*
- *"We can schedule a follow-up in two weeks, or you can self-refer earlier."*

## Voice notes (delete before finalizing)

While drafting, hold these in mind:

- **Stage directions** are *spice* — 5-8 maximum across the whole transcript. Best uses: marking a *pause* during Act 2 when the patient is processing, marking a *long silence* after a hard truth, signaling something the patient does that the words don't capture.
- **Speaker labels** in bold caps style: `**Dr. Sigmund:**` and `**{Patient Name}:**`.
- **Dr. Sigmund's voice**: warm, professional, sometimes literary, never twee, never quippy. He does not make jokes at the patient's expense. He occasionally offers a quiet observation that lands harder than a joke would (Enola session: *"I noticed."*).
- **Patient's voice**: *theirs*, not generic. If you cannot produce a distinctive voice for the patient, your patient profile in Phase 1 was too thin — go back and read more of their files.
- **Length discipline**: every line earns its place. A short transcript with sharp lines beats a long transcript with filler.
